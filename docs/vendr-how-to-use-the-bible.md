# How to use the ÉIRVOX Emotional Bible (the content gate)

The bible is only as strong as the habit of checking against it. Treat it as a
gate: nothing ÉIRVOX-facing ships until it passes, a page, a listing template, a
caption, a Higgsfield prompt, a line of copy.

## The three-question gate

1. **Both-beats test (bible §1, §4)** , does it deliver BOTH *trust* and *desire*?
   Trust without desire is a warehouse. Desire without trust is every scam
   marketplace. Missing either = not ÉIRVOX.

2. **Embodied-trust test (bible §5, §6)** , is trust *shown through curation and
   presentation*, not *announced* with loud "VERIFIED / 100% AUTHENTIC" badges? If
   the content performs trust instead of embodying it, rework it.

3. **Guardrail test (bible §6)** , does anything cross a NOT line? (junk-marketplace
   tone, hype/drop FOMO, founder/hero on the surface, contempt for buyer or seller,
   cold escrow energy, loud sell.) One crossing = rework, no matter how good it
   looks.

Pass all three → it's ÉIRVOX. Fail any → it's another marketplace wearing the name.

## For AI generation (Higgsfield, image, copy models)

- Feed the model bible §1, §4, §5, §6 as context BEFORE prompting. §6 guardrails
  are what stop drift into sneaker-hype or Facebook-Marketplace tone.
- Generate, then run the three-question gate on the OUTPUT, not just the prompt.
- The dangerous failure mode here is *desire without trust* (looks hyped, feels
  like a scam) or *trust without desire* (looks safe, feels like a warehouse).
  Discard both. ÉIRVOX needs the fusion.

## The object is sacred

When the content features an object (a wheel, a watch, a piece), it must be shot
and presented with reverence , light that loves the material, detail that rewards
close looking. A carelessly presented object is an automatic fail; in ÉIRVOX the
object is the hero, not the brand and not a person.

## Who this binds

Everyone and everything that touches ÉIRVOX: you, any cofounder, any contractor,
any designer, any AI. The bible removes "I thought it looked good" as a defence.

> Trust is the floor. Desire is the ceiling. The object is sacred. The curator is
> invisible but present.
