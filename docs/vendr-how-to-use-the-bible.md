# How to use the Vendr Emotional Bible (the content gate)

The bible is only as strong as the habit of checking against it. Treat it as a
gate: nothing Vendr-facing ships until it passes — a page, a section, a caption,
a Higgsfield prompt, a line of copy.

The single source of feeling is [`vendr-emotional-bible.md`](vendr-emotional-bible.md).
When this document and the bible ever seem to disagree, the bible wins.

## The one feeling it all serves

**Relief. The exhale.** (bible §1) The half-second where a person's guard drops:
*"...oh. I'm safe here. Nothing here wants anything from me."* Vendr is a pocket
of calm in a world that is constantly grabbing.

> If a viewer finishes a piece of Vendr content and feels *calmer than when they
> started*, it worked. If they feel sold to, it failed.

## The three-question gate (bible §9)

Before anything ships, ask the bible's own three questions:

1. **Relief (§1)** — does this leave the viewer *calmer*? Does it ask nothing of
   them? Anything that grabs, hurries, or sells has already failed.

2. **Felt, not stated (§5)** — is the theme *felt*, never announced? The moment we
   say it out loud — "the future of vending," "cleaner," "better," "evolved" — we
   stop being Spielberg and start being an ad. Let the viewer arrive at it alone.

3. **Guardrails (§7)** — does anything cross a NOT line? One crossing = rework, no
   matter how good it looks:
   - NOT "vending machines." NOT convenience, a deal, or price comparison.
   - NOT loud, fun, snacky, or bright. No exclamation marks. No primary colours.
   - NOT corporate. NOT a startup. NOT tech-bro. NOT "smart vending solutions."
   - NOT a hero story. No founder, no face, no name on the surface.
   - NEVER a crowd. NEVER a hard sell. NEVER manipulation dressed as convenience.
   - NOT anti-human or "AI takeover" as a stated message. Serenity is felt, never
     preached.

Pass all three → it's Vendr. Fail any → it's something else wearing the name.

## What Vendr stands against (bible §2, §3)

- **The villain is greed** — vending as extraction, "and the indifference it
  breeds." Not people. Not technology. We answer it with calm, not a lecture.
- **There is no hero, deliberately** (§3). No founder, no face, no name. *"The
  absence is the message."* A clean standard does not need a hero to vouch for
  it; it just has to exist. This is why Vendr should feel **inevitable, not
  invented** (§4).

## The audience (bible §8)

The person who would pay 3.50 for something clean *because* it is clean, and
would never stand there doing mental math against the corner shop. **Premium
because care is premium. Not because expensive.**

## Who this binds

Everyone and everything that touches Vendr: you, any cofounder, any contractor,
any designer, any AI. The bible removes "I thought it looked good" as a defence.

> Mood is law. The product is the shark. The feeling is the film. (bible §9)
